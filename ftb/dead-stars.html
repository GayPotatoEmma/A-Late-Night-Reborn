Dead Stars

"Nereid, Triton, and Phobos─a triumvirate of pranksters wielding the power of frost, fire, and poison, respectively. Using their command of the elements, the trio manipulate the weather on the Occult Crescent, having apparently been tasked with preserving the isle's transplanted flora. These familiars are endowed with sufficient intelligence to carry out their duties, but their language and demeanor are decidedly infantile. Whether this was intentional or simply an aberration resulting from eons of continued service is unclear."

Overview

At the start, Decisive Battle assigns every player a Gravity debuff tethering them to one boss (Phobosic, Tritonic, or Nereidic Gravity). Each player can only deal damage to their assigned boss for the remainder of the encounter. The outer death wall activates shortly after.

⚠ Mismatched debuff: If you receive a Gravity debuff for a boss other than the one you were standing near, move to that boss immediately. Tanks with a mismatch: wait until after Slice 'n' Dice before repositioning.
✦ Tanks: Pull your boss into the middle of the arena before Decisive Battle resolves so that the entire raid can cleave all three bosses with AoE attacks.

Slice 'n' Dice

A conal cleaving tankbuster that requires heavy mitigation.

⚠
Phantom Knight
  • Use Phantom Guard and your 25-second cooldown.
ℹ
All other tanks
  • Use your 40% + 20% + 90-second + 25-second cooldowns.
✦ Phantom Samurai: Shirahadori works as a free invuln for this hit. The timing window is short (4 seconds); be precise.

Three-Body Problem: Phobos (Slimes)

Nereid and Triton transform into slime balls. All players attack Phobos. Gravity debuffs are cleared for this phase.

ℹ Primordial Chaos gives every player either a blue ice or red fire debuff with 1–3 stacks. You must get hit by the opposite colour slime the same number of times as your stack count to cleanse it.
  • Check your debuff colour and stack count.
  • Watch the slimes telegraph their jump positions (Frozen Fallout); follow the opposite-colour slime mentally and plan which waymarks to stand on and in which order.
  • Once your debuff is cleansed, move to a safe spot and take no further hits.
⚠ Wait to accept raises during this mechanic until after the Vengeful Fire / Blizzard III cleaves have resolved.

Jumping Cleaves

After every Three-Body Problem phase, the two previously untargetable bosses reappear and jump somewhere in the arena. Each casts a conal cleave in front of itself. The safe area is between the two bosses. Watch where they land and move into the gap.

⚠ If you received a raise before the cleaves happen, wait to accept it. Accepting too early will either kill you or add Thrice-come Ruin stacks.

Delta Attack & Firestrike

Delta Attack is a heavy triple raidwide requiring full mitigation and shields from healers.

Firestrike is a line stack on a random player. Point the stack out of the arena, away from the other groups, to avoid clipping them.

Three-Body Problem: Nereid (Snowballs)

Phobos and Triton become snowballs on the north and south-west of the arena. All players attack Nereid.

ℹ Snowball Flight, line charges: Each snowball performs 3 line charges through the arena (6 total). Every charge must be intercepted by a party with a tank at the front.
  • The first player in each line takes a mini-tankbuster; tanks use their 25-second cooldown.
  • Each hit inflicts Physical Vulnerability Up. You cannot take two consecutive hits or you will die.
  • Every charge includes a knockback. You can use knockback immunity to maintain orientation.

Chilling Collision, tether knockback:

  • One player per snowball is tethered with a stack marker. Use the knockback from the center. Tethered players must be knocked in the direction directly opposite their snowball.
  • Use the arena lines as guidance. Half the raid knocks one direction, half the other.
  • Do not use knockback immunity or stand at the edge; you need the full travel distance.
  • Land on the wall together and stack up using the floor lines. The snowball jumps onto the stack; use burst damage immediately.
✦ Phantom Samurai: Shirahadori can negate a line hit or the stack hit. Using it for the stack is recommended in case you landed too close.
⚠ Wait to accept raises during this mechanic until after the Vengeful Fire / Bio III cleaves have resolved.

Three-Body Problem: Triton (Fireballs)

Phobos and Nereid become targetable fireballs. All players attack Triton.

ℹ Tower spawns: Circular arrow markers appear on the arena floor. Each moves one position counter-clockwise (1/6th of the arena); a tower spawns where the arrow lands, always on a marker line. Fireballs become targetable once towers appear.
  • Tanks: stand in the inner half of the tower (closer to the centre) to avoid DPS and healer cleaves. Use your 25-second cooldown for hit 1, then invuln for hits 2 and 3.
  • DPS: stack on the line between the tower and the outer wall.
  • Healers: stand on the sides, 90° from the DPS, to avoid overlapping cleaves or clipping the other arena side.

Elemental Impact ×3, then circle AoEs:

  • The fireball spits large fire cleaves toward the closest healer and closest DPS.
  • After the 3rd cleave, stack together to bait 4 circle AoEs (Geothermal Rupture); move them clockwise around the fireball, then position for the Flame Thrower line stack outside.
  • The full sequence repeats for a 2nd set. Different tanks take the towers the second time.
✦ Phantom Knight: Can take both tower sets by using Pledge for the first set and saving invuln for the second.
ℹ If you miss your assigned fireball, commit to the closest one and resolve from there. Sprint is recommended for getting into position quickly.
⚠ Wait to accept raises during this mechanic until after the Vengeful Blizzard / Bio III cleaves have resolved.

Soft Enrage: Six-Handed Fistfight

All three bosses merge into a single entity. The inner ring of the arena becomes permanently unsafe.

  • Dodge the pizza-slice cleaves (Collateral Damage): alternating wedges of the star pattern.
  • Reposition towards where you fought your boss:
    • A, B, C move clockwise from their position.
    • 1, 2, 3 move counter-clockwise from their position.
  • Spread around your wedge.
ℹ The image below shows one example spread for a single party. Other parties resolve the same pattern in their respective wedges.

How Do We Resolve Mechanics?

Markers are placed on the 6 lines of the arena floor. Letter and number groups are differentiated to indicate snowball knockback directions using the 4 and D markers.

Which group attacks which boss:

  • A & 1: left
  • B & 2: middle (north)
  • C & 3: right

Snowball line order:

  • Northern Snowball: A → B → C
  • South-West Snowball: 1 → 2 → 3

Snowball knockback directions:

  • A, B, C: knock towards letters (4 or D marker side).
  • 1, 2, 3: knock towards numbers (4 or D marker side).
  • The tethered player adjusts so they are knocked away from their assigned snowball.

Fireball positioning:

  • Fireballs spawn on a marker line and rotate one position counter-clockwise.
  • Letter groups position to the A marker; number groups to the 1 marker, then stay or shift one marker left/right to resolve.
  • DPS on the line toward the wall; Healers on the sides.
  • Tank 1 takes towers first: mitigate hit 1, invuln hits 2–3.
  • Tank 2 takes towers second: mitigate hit 1, invuln hits 2–3.
  • Circle AoEs are baited on the DPS line and moved clockwise around the fireball.
